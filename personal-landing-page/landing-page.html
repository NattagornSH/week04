The Origins of Matcha

Matcha traces its roots to Tang Dynasty China (618–907 AD), where dried tea leaves were ground into fine powder and whisked with hot water. Buddhist monks later introduced this practice to Japan in the 12th century, forever changing the country's relationship with tea.

In Japan, matcha was refined and elevated into an art form, becoming the heart of Chado — the Way of Tea. This ceremonial practice, spread widely by the monk Eisai who brought high-quality tea seeds from China, emphasized beauty in simplicity, stillness, and presence in the moment.

The Art of the Tea Ceremony

In Japan, the tea ceremony is a lifelong discipline — every gesture, from folding the cloth to whisking the bowl, is practiced with deliberate precision. Schools like Urasenke have preserved these traditions for over 400 years, treating each cup of matcha not as a drink, but as a complete expression of mindfulness and respect.
